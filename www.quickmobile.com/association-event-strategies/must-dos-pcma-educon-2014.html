Must-Do’s at PCMA EduCon 2014

by QuickMobile
June 19, 2014

In a few days, leading meeting professionals from around the world will be gathering in Toronto, Ontario for PCMA’s 2014 Education Conference.

At this annual June event, #meetingprofs discover new ideas and solutions for their meeting success and professional development. Since this conference is smaller than Convening Leaders, it also offers amazing networking opportunities in some pretty spectacular Toronto hotspots (Distillery District anyone?). So what are some of the new and interesting things you must check out at EduCon? These ones top our list:

1. Get ahead with the mobile app

With the official PCMA 2014 EduCon App, you’ll have everything you need to find the sessions that interest you, create a personalized itinerary, meet new attendees and old friends, schedule onsite meetings, share your thoughts on social, download session handouts and more – all from the palm of your hand.

It’s available for both Apple and Android devices, so be sure to download it and you’ll be able to hit the ground running once you get to the convention center. QuickMobile Event App Coaches will be on hand with a help desk at registration in case you have any app questions, praise or just want to say hi!

2. Reboot your brain

This year’s general sessions are packed with delicious “brain food”. The first one is with author and Harvard Business Review contributor, Dr. Heidi Grant Halvorson, and focuses on how to achieve your goals using scientifically backed strategies. In another session, Josh Linker will help you discover how to unleash your creative, original thinking and apply it towards your meeting success. And last but not least, Luke Williams shows us the power of disruptive thinking in the events space.

3. Share your wisdom

At its heart, PCMA EduCon inspires event professionals to fall deeper in love with their careers and we couldn’t think of a better place to ask them: What advice do you have for future event planners?
Keep your answer in mind and you might get the chance to share it on camera! Our trusty reporter (and Content and Social Media maven), Karra Barron, will be onsite asking event planners for their words of wisdom.

4. Disrupt Tuesday

PCMA is all about taking risks and this year, they’re shaking up their conference format. Don’t get attached to Monday’s traditional breakout rooms because they’ll be gone by Tuesday (aka Day 2)! Instead, all attendees will gather in one giant room to learn and collaborate together on a variety of different topics. It’s an interesting experiment that will hopefully inspire attendees to come up with their own unique meeting approaches.

5. Design your conference experience

In keeping with the collaborative and innovative nature of Day 2, attendees will be able to use the official event app to suggest and upvote topics for the day.

6. Ottawa tourism tweet up

Before you head to the closing reception, be sure to check out this not-to-be-missed tweet up! It’s not only a pre-party to the reception, but also a pre-celebration to Canada’s birthday on July 1st. Meet your fellow tweeps, re-discover your love of Canada, and see the latest social stats on PCMA EduCon!

See you all at EduCon!
