On the Road: GBTA

by Kerry J. Plowman
August 4, 2016  | 

The Global Business Travel Association (GBTA) Convention wrapped up in Denver last week. “Balance” was the theme of this year’s conference and it examined the role of the travel manager as it continues to evolve and as technology continues to drive business traveler preferences and behavior.

Things started up with the opening reception, “Under the Stars and on the Field”, at Mile High Stadium. The conference attendees filled the stadium where they danced, ate, drank, sang, and enjoyed a fun-filled evening complete with skydivers, live music and a vast choice of delicious foods.

The conference luncheons provided some keen insights from Julia Louis-Dreyfus (a.k.a. Elaine), Peyton Manning and Scott Kelly as they described their incredible journeys that have led them to where they are today. Attendees were given valuable perspective on the challenges that these celebrities had overcome and were provided with important lessons for business and life.

The Center Stage speakers and panelists offered attendees exclusive insight into the airline industry, safety and security, industry consolidation, and the future of ground transportation—as well as the business travel industry as a whole.

Convention attendees were treated to 80+ industry educational sessions. The impact of technology on business travel was the topic of many sessions during the conference. These technology sessions ranged in subject from the creation of mobile app strategy, to the future of technology for everyone. Some of the key learnings included the importance of building a mobile app strategy in a cohesive and non-siloed manner and focusing in on technologies that provide value to users — when and how they need it the most.

QuickMobile was a proud sponsor of the GBTA and provided the Convention mobile app. In true fashion of the digital age, attendees can still use the conference app to download presentations, provide session feedback and connect with other attendees. See you at GBTA 2017!
