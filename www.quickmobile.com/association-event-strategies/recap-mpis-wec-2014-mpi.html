Recap of MPI’s WEC 2014 – Are You MPI?

by QuickMobile
August 13, 2014

They came, they saw, they had no paper! This year for the first time, MPI hosted their 2014 World Education Congress (WEC) in a paperless format and at QuickMobile we are proud to be the partner that helped them make this a viable alternative. This is our fourth year as their event app partner, and being able to showcase an app with so many rich features for connecting and learning is something we love to see come to life. We had many positive comments from the participants using it during the conference as we assisted in the registration area, and later as they visited us in our MarketSquare space.

The most wonderful thing about attending an event of this magnitude and stature is the opportunity to see so many clients and partners, and to meet new people and share stories of how meetings are evolving and how we can be part of this process. With even richer analytics than in previous years, we are able to track the social sharing as well as user experiences throughout the four days. This allows event organizers to make decisions and see what is happening on a continuous basis – something we haven’t always been able to do so easily, and one that will surely benefit meetings moving forward.

There were many highlights, from excellent keynote sessions including Deepak Chopra, who was a draw for many. Flashpoint sessions are always fun, as three presenters each have 15 minutes to share their stories, and provide a glimpse into their deeper dive sessions to come later that day. From Laura Schwarz reminding us to focus when “It’s Showtime” and her lessons learned as the White House Planner during the Clinton administration, to Roel Friessen and Ruud Janssen introducing Event Model Generation, to Nolan Bushnell sharing his stories (including NOT investing in Apple) – it made the selections difficult when it came to choosing your breakouts to attend! The choices were immense, the sessions represented a broad cross-section of interests, and the response and discussions showed time and again they had done a great job. The polls, surveys, SpeakOut and social sections of the app showed how this community embraced the mobile tool and real-time feedback always makes us better.

Minneapolis put its best foot forward with two fantastic evening events. The opening was held on the banks of the Mississippi below the Mill Museum, and the closing on Peavey Square beside the Orchestra Hall. The popular Big Deal and Rendezvous continued in their success of supporting the Foundation, and judging by the in-app gallery photos and social sharing, these were indeed enjoyed!

For the nerd in all of us, San Francisco hosted a lunch on the final day that had Steve Wozniak being interviewed, and his sheer enthusiasm for how technology improves our lives and simply living life was infectious.

Trevor, still high from meeting Steve, carried on to the cool space MPI’s Jessie States had created on the lower level with campfires, tech hubs, career counselling and the MPI library, where he presented a fantastic session on How to Leverage Crowdsourcing and Mobile Apps for a Greater Event Experience via event apps (as seen in action at this year’s WEC) to a captive audience.

We had a ton of fun in our booth as guests chose what made them “MPI” and we took dozens of pictures and tweeted and shared and laughed throughout. If you stopped by our booth, you can find your photo on our Facebook page. This is one of the reasons we meet – to put ideas into context, to learn and share and to build connections through fun. Thank you MPI.
