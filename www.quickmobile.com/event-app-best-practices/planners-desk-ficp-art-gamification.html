From the Planner’s Desk: FICP and The Art of Gamification

by QuickMobile
December 19, 2014

The Financial and Insurance Conference Planners (FICP) have several meetings throughout the year, always focused on education for this savvy group of planners. The recent Annual Conference at the Hilton Waikaloa Resort on the Big Island of Hawaii was no exception. This location was long anticipated by both planners and hospitality partners, as well as the Islands themselves, many who took advantage of hosting planners on pre and post extensions to explore the many options for their groups. This epitomizes the business of meetings, as the planners took advantage of the proximity to maximize the time, and further educate themselves on the destination.

The education sessions offered a plethora of choice and I was excited to talk about two of my favorite subjects, gamification and new meeting technology. FICP had CollectiveNext capture the ideas in the Meeting Technology sessions on graphic storyboards, a true art as the artists have to listen to the presentation and the participants while capturing all the content on the fly. The results offer a great visual story and takeaway ideas for those who were not able to attend the session.

As much as I love technology and its ability to connect humans and drive learning, I equally love low-tech solutions done beautifully to assist learning.

The focus of my gamification session was to create a deeper understanding of what it is, why it works, how to do it, and just as important, how to talk about it internally to create excitement around the delivery of a gamified experience.

What is it? Applying game design principles of clear goals, clear rules, responsive feedback loops and voluntary participation to non-game contexts, such as meetings, trade shows and events.

Why does it work? Simply, human beings learn best through play.  We do not learn best by sitting and listening to someone talk at us for 45 – 90 minutes. When we can learn something through play, we are better able to retain the information and learn how to apply it to other situations.

How can you do it? Using the tools already embedded in a mobile app, which now resides in the pockets and purses of the majority of people attending meetings, points are received for a variety of “good” meeting behaviors. This can include sharing their opinions via polls and surveys; meeting people including other participants, sponsors, exhibitors or board members as examples; exploring spaces they might not otherwise get to on a trade show floor or marketplace environment and generally creating new connections between people and ideas.

What if you want to build team spirit instead of just recognizing individuals? Collaborative gamification – where the collective is working to achieve points is a powerful tool and meets intrinsic needs for satisfaction, fun, belonging, meaning and autonomy in participation. Real-life examples we have seen include collective “happiness” points to drive positive behaviors, earning points for acts of “green” that taught the group how to produce more sustainable meetings (with each act counting towards a total charitable donation amount), and shared ideas that not only earn points but also create a fantastic collective pool of knowledge and ideas shared in-app for consumption both during and after the meeting, extending your story.

If your bigger task is to sell the idea of gamification internally, it is about beginning with your SMART objectives, and clearly articulating what you want your participants to leave with. Mobile app gamification can help if you want to get engaged attendees more actively participating in the experience, draw out ideas from quieter people as they have an alternate forum to thoughtfully express themselves, create a stronger sense of connection between participants, give people reasons to meet (“I saw you on the leaderboard”), and/or allow people to learn new tasks in the safe environment of a game, where you can fail, try again and master concepts. When you add gamification as part of your core design it will allow you to achieve greater overall success!

It is always a pleasure to be part of an FICP event. Their commitment to strong meeting design, allowing opportunities for relationships to be strengthened at thoughtfully planned events, and an overall sense of joie de vivre in what we do has built a community of people committed to the meeting and incentive industry—and this is something unique, and embraceable. I look forward to a return engagement.
