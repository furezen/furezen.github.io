4 Steps For Choosing the Right Event Technology

by Kristen Carvalho
June 15, 2015  | 

When the time comes for you and your business to invest in technology for your event, what will you choose? Will you go with the registration software that your client recommended? Will you pick the event app with the most appealing interface? Or will you just call it a day and pick the most talked about new technology for engagement?

Purchasing event technology is a big investment of your company’s money and your team’s time. This is not a hasty decision that can be made with one phone call; you need to do your research. Let’s look at a few things to consider to ensure that you are getting the right event tech for your event!

Goals

Before you get started on your search, you need to know what goals you want to achieve. There are many different types of event technology and many different reasons that event professionals would need to invest in a solution. Are you looking to increase your registrations with a more efficient system? Do you want to engage your audience better onsite? Are you looking for publicity? There are so many different ways that you can go with event technology; you simply need to know what is right for you.

Just because you see articles or reports on the hottest trends like social walls or iBeacon technology, does not mean you need it for you event. Don’t get something just because it’s cool and everyone else is doing it. Make sure if you are investing money in purchasing the technology that it will actually help you increase your event’s effectiveness. Stick to the technology that you know you will use for more than one of your events and may even help you with your event efforts year round! Remember, you also have to take the time to get trained on this tech, so don’t waste your time with “what’s hot” if it doesn’t make sense for your goals.

Integrations

Once you know what your goals are, find a company that plays well with others. Chances are this is not going to be the only piece of technology that you will use to help increase your event’s efficiency. Find a solution that seamlessly integrates with other software providers that you are currently using or may want to implement down the road.

Data is extremely important and it can be time consuming to import and export information between two systems. For example, if you implemented an audience engagement tool at your event through your app, wouldn’t you want that to directly connect to your registration software and CRM system? That way you can see what content attendees liked best – without lifting a finger! Look for a system with those integrations or an opened API to transfer data.

Reporting

Another important feature in technology is reporting. You are going to be investing thousands of dollars into event technology for you event, so you better make sure the investment is worth it! Most software will have some type of reporting functionality, but you want to make sure that it is robust and will help you measure the goals that you laid out in the beginning. Look for a robust real-time dashboard that will let you see minute to minute how your investment is doing. Even better, try and see if this event tech has a professional services team or account manager that can walk you through all the reporting features and help you figure out the best ways to get the results you want.

Research

Now that you know what you are looking for, where do you find it? You need to do more than just research online. Obviously you can visit a few websites and take a few free trials, but you want to hear from some real people too. There are a few ways to do this.

First, start by attending tradeshows. The whole reason that companies exhibit at tradeshows is to gain new business. The sales representatives onsite are knowledgeable (well, hopefully) about the product they are selling. This is a great opportunity to pit a few different options against each other. Make a plan before you go on who you want to see and have a list of the same questions to ask each company (make sure at least a few connect to your goals). See who is able to meet all or most of the things on your list.

Also start looking at review sites for feedback from current and former clients. See the good and the bad of the company to get an idea of what you are in for. In terms of software, there are two websites that are a great place to look for reviews – Capterra and G2Crowd. These review websites are dedicated to business software reviews and are great places to see the other options that are out there!

Don’t expect this process to take a few days, this is a big decision and you need to make sure you are making the right one! Expect to spend a few weeks doing your research and debriefing with your team on what you think the best options are. You will know when you find the right fit for your needs. Let’s just hope it’s in time for your event!
