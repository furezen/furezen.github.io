On Site at IRF’s Annual Education & Incentive Invitational

by Kerry J. Plowman
June 15, 2016  | 

On the heels of another great conference, the IRF invitational provided the incentive travel industry an opportunity to network and frolic in the heat of a Las Vegas summer for the 22nd year in a row!

The conference itself began with an education day where attendees learned about the 2016 trends of the incentive industry, followed by insights from the hotel and airline industries and even a discussion with panel of millennials (including QuickMobile’s own Sheila Franjkovic). Lessons about behaviour economics, incentive buying and plenty of “peeks behind the curtains” filled the afternoon program. The education day’s closing keynote was delivered by Carey Lohrenz, a former F-14 Tomcat pilot and author of “Fearless Leadership: High-Performance Lessons from the Flight Deck.”

And in the spirit of incentive travel, some fun was bound to be had with all the informative talks! Day 2 of the conference was a chance to let people get out to various areas of Vegas and enjoy an exciting activity of their own choice, making this a true incentive conference.

Some of the great activities for attendees of this year’s conference included:

  • A 4-player scramble golf tournament at the prestigious Wolf Golf course – one of the longest in Nevada offering a total of 7,604 yards with a par 72 layout. It was voted as one of the “Fifty Places to Play Golf Before You Die” by Chris Santella.

  • The Richard Petty Ride-Along and Rookie Racing Experience. The IRF attendees with fire in their belly and heavy right foot got to zip around the track in a 600 horsepower NASCAR racecar, taking the driver’s seat for 8 laps around the Las Vegas Motor speedway in nothing short than a white-knuckle experience.
  • For the artistic attendees, the Paint and Pour experience provided the budding artists with a glass of wine and a fresh white canvas to pour their creativity with step-by-step instructions by a true artist.
  • The explorers of the conference had the choice of guided hiking through the Red Rock canyon or exploring the magic of Vegas in the Downtown Las Vegas Crawl, visiting the Mob Museum, the Neon Museum and more!
  • The conference wound down with a casual Cabana day for all attendees to pull out their beach gear, participate in fun activities, live music and networking all day.

This year’s conference was quite exceptional as there was almost literally a 50-50 split between hosted buyers and sellers. This made for some very lively conversation and plenty of opportunities for people to take notes, make friends, talk shop and learn the latest in incentive travel trends.

QuickMobile was a Gold Sponsor of the event and the official app provider. It gave us the opportunity to talk to people and find out how our product can support those who have incentive programs as well as those who supply them. One of the key learnings was the fact that the travel market is getting more and more tech dependant and the digital-loving millennials are going to be expecting tech in new and different areas of their upcoming incentive trips. QuickMobile will be here to keep them and the IRF at the forefront of technology as we forge into the future.

Thanks IRF for another great conference, it has been an honor being your partner for the last 4 years!
