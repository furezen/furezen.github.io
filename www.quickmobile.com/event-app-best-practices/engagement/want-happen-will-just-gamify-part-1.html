If You Want It to Happen, It Will. Just Gamify It. (Part 1 of 2)

by QuickMobile
June 21, 2016

No matter what type of gathering you’re organizing, you know going in what you want to accomplish and what you want your participants to walk away with.

But how do you motivate your audience to accomplish what you want them to and measure your results?

Gamification is the ideal strategy for accomplishing virtually anything at events, making it possible to drive specific behaviors by awarding points for completing specific activities. Some events award prizes to winners who appear at the top of the event app leaderboard, whereas others may focus on more intrinsic values to motivate attendees. Either way, with the right motivators in place for your audience, just imagine what you could accomplish with a little friendly competition.

Now that most enterprise event app platforms have built-in gamification, it’s getting easier to pull off a successful game. All you need to do is choose the right app components to help drive the behavior you’re looking for. Once you try it, you’ll find that gamification can really propel you towards achieving all of your objectives.

To give you a start, here are 5 key objectives you can achieve with gamification at your events:

1) Improve networking

How many events have you attended where a small handful of attendees are networking and the rest are sitting quietly in the corner? Gamification changes everything. When you offer game points for exchanging contact information through the event app, you can transform a quiet conference into a much more vibrant, social event, bringing even the most introverted of attendees into the conversation.

2) Increase visits to exhibitor booths

Keeping exhibitors happy with a constant stream of foot traffic is every event organizer’s dream. By offering game points for checking in at exhibitor booths, you’re giving attendees a real incentive to break the ice and start conversations with exhibitors.

3) Get more feedback after sessions

Offering convenient surveys in the event app may not always be enough to get the feedback you want from a session or speaking engagement. But when you reward attendees with game points for completing surveys, you’ll likely get more feedback than you ever anticipated. You can also extend your surveys beyond sessions to virtually any topic surrounding your event such as your hospitality, services or overall event impressions.

4) Fill up more seats

When you gamify session attendance, the empty seats that remain at the back of the lecture halls often fill up with attendees. Even better, when you wait until the end of the session to present your attendees with a PIN or QR code to show their attendance rather than the beginning, you’re almost guaranteed to keep every seat in the house filled until the end.

5) Improve learning retention

A great way to keep people focused and engaged during a session is to forewarn them that a quiz is coming at the end of the session and that points will be awarded for successful completion. Quizzes can be easily activated at the end of a session to make sure no one gets a head start.

Which key event objectives have you accomplished with an event app game? How did the game transform your event?

Stay tuned for next week’s blog where we cover 5 more key objectives that you can accomplish with gamification.
