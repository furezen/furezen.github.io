Extending the Event Lifecycle through Mobile Technology

by Hannah Kaeter
March 21, 2014

The goal of every event planner is to put on the best event possible, to come away knowing the event was a wild success. What makes this happen? Sessions running as scheduled? Great food? No technical difficulties? These victories are all well and good, but the most important measure of success is the experience of your attendees. If you want your attendees to attend your event next year, they have to see the value in attending for much more than a day, says event consultant, William Thomson.

Face-to-face events are important to attendees for many reasons. Forrester Research estimates that approximately two-thirds of senior executives go to industry conferences to learn about new products and services. They also attend to meet other leaders, share ideas and develop productive relationships.

Attendees also want (and expect) to be engaged participants, not just passive recipients. “Adept at using social networking sites such as Twitter, LinkedIn and Facebook, meeting attendees are accustomed to receiving information real-time, and to engaging in continuing dialogue,” according to Espirit Production’s white paper.

But you already know all this, right? That’s the reason you are planning this event to begin with! So let’s dive into how to make this happen. How can you fully engage your attendees to ensure they have a fantastic event experience? Read below for tips on engagement through mobile technology before, during and after events.

Before the event

Crowdsource using pre-event surveys

  • Find out what matters to your attendees, so you can ensure you are providing them with the most relevant content possible. Ask them for input on session content, activities, structure, learning style preferences, or anything else that may help you better shape your event.

Promote via social media

  • Pick an event hashtag for your attendees to use when they reference the event. Remember that short and simple works best.
  • Use all the great channels and mediums at your disposal to hype up your event and also let attendees (or future attendees) know about their opportunities for input. For example, send out a Tweet of an update like: “Going to #EventHashtag? Visit (your link here) to have your say on session content, activities, presentation preferences & more!”

Encourage attendees to build profiles

  • Whether through your event app (best case), or through your website, enable attendees to set up their profiles before the event, so they can begin networking and identifying people they want to meet on-site.

During the event

Kick your event app into full gear

  • Provide all of your event information, like agendas, bios, floor plans, personal notepad, attendee profiles, exhibitor list, social media and more through your app so attendees can easily access it all from their smartphone or tablet. Your event app also further facilitates networking, allowing attendees to easily capture each other’s contact information and chat with one another.

Utilize real-time polling and messaging in session

  • Whether built-in to your event app or not, interactive polling and messaging will ensure your attendees are continuously engaged and included throughout sessions.

Connect further using social media

  • Embed links to event-specific Twitter feeds, Facebook or LinkedIn pages within your event app, making it easier than ever for attendees to share and view posts.
  • Consider having a visual wall of your event hashtag’s Twitter feed somewhere central in your venue. It’s engaging and adds a fun ‘wow’ factor.

After the event

Crowdsource using post-event surveys

  • Get feedback on anything and everything so you can make plans to improve next year’s event.

Share results

  • Keep the content from you event top of mind by communicating interesting findings from the data collected via surveys, polls, and social media (before, throughout and after) with your attendees. Send a push notification via your event app to notify attendees that this information is available right on their device.

Don’t let up on social media!

  • Post a photo gallery of the event to Facebook or your website, and share via your social media channels.
  • Share event recaps and interesting statistics that came out of the event in the weeks and months following your event.
