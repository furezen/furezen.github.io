10 Ways Tech Trends are Impacting Meetings & Events

by Dan Berger
August 20, 2015  | 

As the connected world continues to grow—today, there are 5.2 billion mobile phone users in the world, representing at 72% population penetration; in 1995, that number was 80 million, or 1% penetration—what impact can we expect this growth to have on our industry?

At this year’s PreVue Visionary Summit in Orlando, I had the opportunity to share my thoughts on how this and other technological trends are actively shaping the future of the meetings and events industry. Here is a look at what to expect as the mobile world continues to scale:

1. Sharing Economy

Everything a planner will need to run an event will be available on demand (e.g. riggers, security, check-in staff, porta potties)

  • Real-Life Application: eLance allows planners immediate access to contracted event staff. Chewse delivers customized catering in seconds. Luxe offers on-demand parking and valet services.

2. Mesh Networks

With growing concerns over privacy online, more events will happen for discussing sensitive issues and mesh networks (independent internet networks) will be used to operate them.

3. The Dissolution of Hierarchical Structure

The Internet allows people’s demands to bubble up from the bottom so crowdfunding events and choosing meeting agendas will eventually come directly from attendees.

  • Real-Life Application: Companies like Lumi allow you to poll attendee’s via an app on their smartphones or tablets to help you capture their in-the-moment ideas instantaneously.

4. Mobility

This doesn’t only mean the second screen is here to stay; it means attendees will be more trackable thanks to the sensors their devices will have. That means more measurable event ROI for marketers.

5. Robots

Drones will be used to map meeting space, photograph events, and measure activity. Machines, not humans, will be setting up and tearing down rooms and exhibit halls, checking us in, and delivering us room service.

  • Real-Life Application: Two major hotel chains are already testing out robots in their properties: IHG’s Room Service Robot and Starwood’s Robotic Butler.

6. The Internet as a Utility

More people being online means more people can find out about events. Increased event demand will mean an increase in supply.

7. Cyber Security

Areas were lots of people congregate (e.g. event networks and hotels) will be the target of saboteurs and hackers.

  • Real-Life Application: Guests at properties worldwide are already at risk for hacking, because many properties are dependent upon routers to distribute their Wi-Fi.

8. Artificial Intelligence

Certain components of administrative tasks such as travel booking and meeting planning will be automated.

9. Internet of Things (IOT)

In the future, everything will be online. Imagine a smart conference room table that takes notes and facilitates a meeting or a chair that gently wakes you up if you’re you’re losing concentration at a meeting.

  • Real-Life Application: MIT and Marriott collaborated on Six Degrees, “a physical social network” that links guests’ phones to one another via a “matchmaking table” and screens throughout Marriott’s lobbies to connect travelers via LinkedIn.

10. Privacy

Less personal privacy and more available data will mean events will be even more targeted and the data they collect will be even more coveted.
