The Benefits of Mobile Apps to Event Team Communication

by QuickMobile
January 31, 2014

Where would we be without email and mobile phones? Some say we’d be a lot happier and less stressed about work – maybe even more productive – but for event planning teams, going without is simply not an option. Even if face-to-face meetings took place every day, the pace of event planning and the details involved could never be adequately covered without the need for phone calls, texts and emails.

If you’re part of an event planning team managing dozens of events a year, you rely heavily on these and other tools to keep yourself organized and get things done. As much as we might say we hate email, it’s pretty hard to imagine doing our job without it. The same goes for mobile phones, which is where we’ve been dealing with most of our email for a while now anyway.

But like the internal combustion engine and cars, there’s one element of event planning that hasn’t evolved to nearly the same degree as other technologies around it: the dreaded event binder.

Moving from paper to digital

Despite its obvious shortcomings in our increasingly digital world, the event binder has remained virtually unaffected and unchanged, anchoring (or is it weighing down?) the event planning process like the paper ballot anchors democracy.

From a team communications perspective alone, the binder is a relic that can actually put events at risk. Consider its core purpose: to serve as the keeper of all relevant and up-to-date contracts, floor plans, orders, contact lists, and more. Doesn’t it seem a trifle odd that a planning team depends on a paper-based binder to keep tabs on a project as large, complex and fluid as a major event? What of all the details contained in emails, including BEOs, proposals, contracts and other critical documents? What of all the conversations and notes passing back and forth between members of the planning team? How can a binder possibly stay current?

The simple answer is, it cannot. That’s why tools like email and text messages have become so important, and why more tech-savvy teams have started using web-based platforms such as Dropbox and Evernote to create and share event plans. These platforms have been embraced for their ability to offer secure, on-demand access to key information across the team and beyond. When paired with email and other mobile technologies such as shared calendars, it’s easy to predict the demise of the dreaded binder.

But before you go headlong into this direction, assembling a collection of disparate mobile-ready tools to overcome the challenges you face with your event binder, consider for a moment the impact on your team of introducing, rolling out and training on these disconnected platforms. Then consider what you will need to do to encourage actual use. Replacing a single binder with one, two or more separate tools might not be the best solution.

5 reasons team communications need to go mobile

The answer lies in a dedicated, mobile-ready platform designed with the needs of event planning in mind. Such a platform can unite a large and geographically distributed team, ensuring everyone has access to vital information and resources no matter where they are and no matter when they want to use them. It becomes the locus for all event-specific communication and collaboration, serving as a virtual binder while offering far more flexible and powerful tools for the event planning process. Here are some ways a mobile event planning app can help.

  1. Instant messaging for the event planning team: Text messages get lost in the clutter of phone-based messaging. Better to share notes and messages with other members of the planning team directly within the app. You also benefit from automatic message archiving for easy, perpetual access.
  2. Shared documents: Upload once and voilà! Easily store and share critical event related documents for instant access by any app user; use any file format you need, all viewable on a tablet or smartphone.
  3. Global and personal calendars: Keep track of all event planning milestones even as they shift; allow each functional group and team member to build their own task-related calendars for quicker reference
  4. Contact lists: Imagine the convenience of a flexible contact management system that lets you organize and sort contacts by type (e.g. internal and external) and function (e.g. creative team, food & beverage team, venue & accommodations team, etc.); track vital contact information, as well as specific details such as on-site contact and hotel information.
  5. Information booth: You often have global information about the destination, weather, travel requirements, general FAQ’s and more that need a place to live without getting lost amidst the ebb and flow of event-specific information. Keep it all in one easy-to-access folder within the app, separate from the mission-critical details.

A mobile event planning app addresses all the needs of your planning process safely and efficiently, providing a convenient platform for communication and collaboration among your team members. It can unshackle you from the limitations and frustrations of the traditional paper binder, allowing you to keep everyone involved in the loop and up to date. Plus it has the advantage of being more accessible and usable than its paper-based ancestor.
