Event Planner’s Guide to Catching a Break

Sneak some downtime into the holidays

by Grace Cheung
December 21, 2016

The holidays are one of the busiest times of the year, especially for event planners. Convincing yourself you can afford to take a break can be hard.

Festivities are going on all around you: sleigh bells are ringing, people are wearing red hats. The song that keeps playing in your local Starbucks is starting to make you seriously contemplate if Frosty really will be back again someday. Everybody wants to throw a party, whether in your personal or professional life—and that means your calendar and to-do lists are filled up to the brim.

There’s actually a lot of research that says taking a break can boost productivity, but sometimes the need to talk to just one more venue manager can overpower the desire to take a short walk to the water cooler.

For those of you trying to find time between playing phone tag and arguing with the caterer (of course you need gluten-free vegan options, what is this, 2004?) to go to the bathroom, much less take a break, here are 5 ways to sneak some downtime into your busy (holi)day:

 

1. Pace

If there’s a call you absolutely have to take, and if this keeps happening throughout the holiday season, you might want to think about taking it standing up. Walk from one end of the meeting room to the other, or up and down the stairs if you’re feeling particularly hale and hearty.

Exercise is proven to improve concentration, memory and mental sharpness. Let your body go through its paces while your mind focuses on the conversation. This allows you to fit some physical activity into your busy day while ticking a few tasks off your list.

Live Strong also suggests chewing gum to improve concentration, but I think we can agree that that’s a significantly less impactful form of physical activity.

 

2. Desk Yoga

Not everyone is lucky enough to have a standing desk, but luckily for all of us, you don’t need to stand to get some stretching in.

This blog by Very Well has a few simple poses that you can do without ever leaving your computer. The Yoga Journal has an entire category of articles on the subject, with short instructional videos. Warning that these are varying levels of weird-to-look-at. Your coworkers might give you odd looks, but at least your shoulders and neck will be nice and relaxed.

If you’re really dedicated, you can probably do some of these while keeping one hand on your smartphone.

 

3. Clean Your Desk(top)

Getting your physical and virtual space organized can go a long way towards improving your mental and physical health—and also help you find that spreadsheet you downloaded weeks ago.

Don’t think about anything else while you’re cleaning; let your mind clear as you right-click delete old files and move things into appropriate folders. Give in to your inner neat-freak and color-code everything. Do what feels right.

Once you can find Important Documents easily, you’ll be less stressed and finish your tasks quicker.

 

4. Have Meetings Offsite

Instead of meeting your colleague or potential supplier in a regular old meeting room, walk with them to a nearby café or bakery and have your talk there (unless, of course, the information is confidential or sensitive).

This one is really the all-purpose solution: you get exercise, a break, caffeine, and work done at the same time. You might even accidentally get to experience some of the festivities happening all around!

 

5. Give Yourself Some Credit

The holidays being as busy as they are, it can be too easy to find ourselves completely overwhelmed. But take a minute, in between catching up on spreadsheets and putting red and green stickers on envelopes, to think on your accomplishments this past year. Allowing yourself to think positively on your own achievements can go a long way to inspiring you with the confidence and motivation to end the holiday season with a bang!

 

Even if you can’t get away from work, there are ways to make sure you stay healthy, sane, and productive over the holidays. If you have any go-to ways to give yourself a breather in this busiest of times, tweet us @QuickMobile or leave a comment below!
