16 Ways To Coat Your Meetings and Events In Green

by QuickMobile
March 28, 2012

An American Express Meetings and Events travel forecast said 80 percent of meeting organizers and hoteliers in North America anticipate increasing or maintaining their current number of in-person meetings through this year compared with 2011. In the United States alone 1.8 million meetings were held in 2010, attracting 205 million people, according to the Convention Industry Council.

That means millions of meetings throughout North America are about to grow increasingly greener, because 73 percent of meeting organizers surveyed in the American Express forecast indicated clients are showing increasing interest in running more environmentally friendly meetings.

Given that people plan to meet nearly as often in person in 2012 as in 2011, here are some ways to green up your events.

1. Provide session video recordings only online or through a mobile app. Zero physical DVDs means no materials, no shipping, no hassle.

2. Consider using a mobile app to virtually replace the annual printed event guide, which in itself eliminates all printing costs, paper costs and distribution costs.

3. Incorporate a green-friendly game to help incentivize the notion of being greener. For example, during the Get Your Green On Event Camp Vancouver 2011 conference, onsite and virtual attendees could play a game throughout the conference designed to promote sustainable behavior. The game involved snapping and uploading to a conference photo gallery pictures of random acts of green living, taking on green challenges for points, and typing in codes hidden throughout the event website, akin to a scavenger hunt. For details of how the game was designed and the results, download this excellent white paper on running a green game at a conference.

4. Create an event to help attendees bond around the notion of environmentally friendly concepts. Green Meeting Industry Councils Sustainable Meetings Conference attendees were asked to as a team assemble a bike to donate to the Boys & Girls Clubs of America.

5. Use e-mail marketing to promote events year-round, instead of glossy fliers in the weeks leading up to the event.

6. Create a mobile application that provides an online repository and fully searchable database for all session videos, speaker notes and other kinds of documents, accessible from any smart phone or tablet. This eliminates the need for stacks of handouts, not to mention the expenses and materials needed to create physical products.

7. Drop traditional advertising methods in favor of newer ways to get your messages across, such as social media and blogging. Use your blog to promote events and create guest posts for blogs whose audience is similar to those at your event or conference. You can also publish announcements in LinkedIn groups frequented by prospects.

8. Exchange wasteful direct mail with a targeted LinkedIn pay-per-click campaign just focused on C-level executives for example, that not only saves physical material, your company only pays when people click.

9. Provide typically through a mobile app an online list of all attendees, to eliminate the need to exchange physical contact information. This can also be done by adding QR codes to name tags. Instead of trading business cards, visitors can scan the codes, automatically adding that persons contact information.

10. Corbin Ball Associates, a firm that integrates technology into events, published a list of excellent resources for greening up meetings as well, including how to manage room blocks at various hotels electronically, to cut down on a lot of faxing.

11. Organizing your electronic tradeshow floor plans can be done through tools like those at http://www.expocad.com. Many event apps pinpoint a particular exhibitor on the floor, and map a GPS-like guided path between himself or herself and the booth, all paper-free.

12. Instead of hauling stacks of white papers and other documentation to meetings, hand out USB drives with all the paperwork on it if there is a desire to provide something physical to each prospect. You could also go one step further by providing a URL or quick response (QR) code individuals can visit or scan to download the information upon returning to the office. Not only is this a greener way to go, but it is less hassle for both the exhibitors and conference attendees.

13. Nix physical paper surveys, and allow people to engage through an app on smart phones and tablets with live polls during the sessions. In addition, provide survey forms electronically, for completion immediately after the session from any smart phone or tablet.

14. Tack on a virtual element to a traditional one. Provide an option to attend sessions online at a reduced rate. This allows each session to have an entire secondary audience online who otherwise would miss out on the gathering, not to mention all the practical and environmental ancillary benefits of avoiding travel.

15. Use analytics on conference apps, in order to figure out what sessions, documents and aspects of the conference are most valuable, as a way to more effectively save and use resources.

16. Companies can create a multi-event apps which can be easily uploaded with information unique to each event, for events that happen repeatedly in different cities, or for large organizations that may host dozens or hundreds of internal meetings. This allows all that data to be uploaded to the same app, thereby eliminating agendas and meeting information hundreds of times over.

As you can see from the list above, many of these sure-fire ways to green-up your events grows out of a purposeful attempt to enhance the experience of attending the conference with only recently available mobile technologies. So not only are you doing the environment good, but you are increasing the value of your events. What could be better than that?
