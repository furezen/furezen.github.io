5 Things Event Profs Can Learn from #QMhackday

by QuickMobile
June 13, 2013

QuickMobile held its first ever Hack-a-Thon– aka #QMhackday – on June 10, 2013. Think tank events like this are a fun and collaborative way to generate new ideas that keep our company innovative and provide our clients with more amazing mobile solutions.

We randomly assigned employees from our technical, creative, sales and even HR departments into teams and asked each one to come up with an idea for a new product or solution in just 8 hours. It was a long day filled with great ideas, interesting challenges and tons of coffee, but the end result was worth it.

There were 14 teams and by the end of the day, we had 14 brilliant ideas. Stay tuned over the coming months to see which ones we decided to bring to life!

That said, as #QMhackday progressed, we noticed a lot of ways event professionals could benefit from doing their own version of a Hack-a-Thon. Here are five different ways you can take advantage of this exercise:

1. Pick the brains of your superstars

Whether you’re a one-man event team in a big organization or part of an events company, there’s a huge benefit to brainstorming with people from other departments.

This is an opportunity to get an entirely different perspective on an idea or problem. Think about it: your HR rep deals with people every day and could have some great insight into what everyone finds exciting right now. That kind of knowledge will help you develop a stronger attendee engagement strategy.

Also, working with someone from a different team gives you a fresh set of eyes. They may see a solution you missed because you’ve been too close to all the planning for your event.

2. Reach your audience and increase registrations

Making people aware of your event is tough. Getting them to actually register can be even tougher. If you’ve tried traditional promotional campaigns (i.e. email blasts, social media posts, newsletter updates) and still struggle to sell out your event, it may be time to try something new.

Dedicating a whole day to idea generation forces you and your team to really think about your events strategy, particularly who your audience is and how you’re going to attract them. Throw out as many ideas as you can – good and bad – and you may just come up with something that will have your attendees practically racing to hit that ‘Register’ button.

3. Tackle the routine processes that make you tear your hair out

Have you ever been working on a tight deadline, but find yourself in dire need of a more efficient way to find sponsors, rent equipment, register attendees and/or book speakers?

It’s easy to try to save time by sticking with a process that kind of works, but if you have to practically pull your teeth out to get the result you want, then it’s not the right strategy for you. Take care of these issues now before they set you or your event back in a bigger way.

Use a Hack-a-Thon as an excuse to put aside your normal duties for a few hours and finally find ways to solve these problems. This is your chance to not only make your events run more efficiently, but make your business better as well.

4. Brainstorm innovative event content

Amazing content is a huge part of why a person decides to attend an event, so the last thing you want to do is give them the same old boring experience. Instead, you should aim to deliver unique content that will have attendees talking up your event long after they’ve left the venue.

A hack day will help your team brainstorm new ways to delight your attendees and enhance their event experience. As you dive into this exercise, look at your event strategy and consider things like: What are attendees going to learn? Who are they going to learn from? What experience do I want to give them?

You may just come out of your think tank session with innovative ideas like a cool mobile event app, one-of-a-kind keynote address or new kind of networking activity.

5. Find ways to decrease event costs

As you plan your event, that big, bad budget will always be looming in the back of your mind. Use your hack day to not only come up with fresh ideas, but to also find ways to make them happen without going over budget.

This is a good time to examine your entire strategy and see where you can cut spending in order to bring to life an idea that will truly make your event stand out.

No matter what kind of event you’re planning, finding the time to just sit and think of ways to be innovative and memorable can be extremely beneficial to your event’s success. Just remember to try this exercise as a team – two (or five or eight) heads are better than one, after all.
