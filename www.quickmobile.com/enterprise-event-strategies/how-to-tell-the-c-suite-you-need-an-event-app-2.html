How to Tell the C-Suite you Need an Event App

by QuickMobile
February 7, 2014

Trying to make a business case for your event app? Read the first post in this “Selling Event Apps to the C-Suite” series here.

According to a new US CEO Study by PriceWaterhouseCoopers, 86% of business leaders surveyed cited technological advances as the global trend that will transform business. In fact, 39% of companies are planning to invest in mobile customer engagement this year. What are the mobile technologies CEO’s are looking at adopting?

“Mobile apps that provide an always-on experience for customers, and enterprise apps that help knowledge workers more effectively do their jobs and are accessed via the company app store.”

If you are looking to deploy an app for your next conference or meeting, you may find yourself having to make your case directly to a C-level executive. The direction for a company’s mobile or event technology strategy can come from four specific offices in the C-suite, and you could be dealing with one, all, or none of these execs.

Should you find that you’re not in the latter situation, here are a few tips on how to sell your case for an event app to your C-suite:

Chief Executive Officer

CEOs are constantly looking for ways to differentiate the company’s products and services from the competition – and this also holds true for the events that they hold. Customer interaction and response are very important to the CEO and events, like user conferences, provide them with a direct touch to their buyers.

As the PWC study shows, US CEOs agree that “technology is what propels business, now and in the future.” If disruptive technology can make your company’s events and meetings stand out and increase business, then CEOs will more readily embrace something like a mobile app.

Make the case to your CEO that an event app will help your company better connect and communicate with your customers. It enhances your event experience, provides new opportunities to showcase your products or services, serves as a conduit for getting real-time feedback from buyers, and keeps your company connected to your attendees year round.

Be sure to also explain how the event app analytics will provide data that the enterprise can use to improve future events, grow the business, and provide intel for innovation in areas like customer service and interaction, and product development.

Chief Marketing Officer

Always at top of mind for your CMO is how the enterprise can drive brand awareness while increasing revenue and growing the business. The CMO is the champion of the digital marketing strategy, but may still struggle to understand how mobile fits into their marketing initiatives.

One of your first challenges will be to help your CMO realize all the ways an event app can further enhance the event experience while still supporting the brand. Start by discussing how the app will meet all or most of your event objectives and include examples of how these also tie back to your overall business goals. You can refer to our post on building a business case for your app for an easy way to do this.

Give your CMO some insight into how you’ll promote the event app without disrupting the event marketing strategy. Cite ways you can even tie your app promotion into your other marketing tactics to drive excitement and registrations. With marketing being more metrics-driven than ever, explain what kind of key data the event app analytics will reveal about your event and attendees.

Chief Financial Officer

PWC found that both net income and revenue have risen in the past three years among the Standard & Poor 500 companies. While cost-control is still important to the CFO, they are now in a better position to adopt new strategies and explore new areas that the business can leverage for growth.

You need to show your CFO that an event app is an investment that can pay for itself over time with the different ways it meets your business objectives (again, using research you conducted when you built your business case) and provides valuable data that can help you make strategic decisions about future events. For example, you’ll be able to tell if 100% of attendees used the app, thereby eliminating the need for printed materials and allowing you to use the money saved on something else.

You should also show your CFO that the event app can literally pay for itself through different monetization options, such as sponsorship. Find out more about event app monetization with this handy guide.

Chief Information Officer

The CIO can be your biggest ally in your event app adoption quest. In fact, the trend will be for companies to reshape their IT departments “to be more agile and consultative to the business.” The CIO is all about enhancing internal and external collaboration through the use of technology. For them, data is the name of the game.

The first step in winning over the CIO is to demonstrate how your mobile event app will help the company stand out from other competitor’s who may be doing the same thing. You will want to talk about the different features your app will have and how you plan on using them to meet your event and business objectives. Be ready to also explain what the process of integrating the event app will be like (you can also opt to have an event app vendor talk directly to your CIO about this).

Your CIO will also need to know about the security of the app and what systems and policies the event app vendors you’ve shortlisted have in place to protect information contained within it. Like your CEO and CMO, the CIO will also be interested in the kind of data the event app can provide, so walk them through the different real-time and post-event analytics you’ll be able to give them (use this guide!).
